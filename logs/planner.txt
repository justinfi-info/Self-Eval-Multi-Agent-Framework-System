**Shot Plan: Calculating Final Price with 10% Discount**

**Scene 1: Introduction**

* Camera angle: Straight-on shot of a whiteboard or calculator
* Action: Write the initial product price ($1000) on the whiteboard or display it on the calculator
* Voiceover: "Let's calculate the final price of a product that costs $1000 with a 10% discount."

**Scene 2: Calculate Discount Amount**

* Camera angle: Close-up shot of the calculator or whiteboard
* Action: Calculate the discount amount by multiplying the initial price by the discount percentage (10% = 0.10)
* Math:
	+ Discount amount = Initial price x Discount percentage
	+ Discount amount = $1000 x 0.10
	+ Discount amount = $100
* Voiceover: "To find the discount amount, we multiply the initial price by the discount percentage. In this case, the discount amount is $100."

**Scene 3: Calculate Final Price**

* Camera angle: Close-up shot of the calculator or whiteboard
* Action: Subtract the discount amount from the initial price to find the final price
* Math:
	+ Final price = Initial price - Discount amount
	+ Final price = $1000 - $100
	+ Final price = $900
* Voiceover: "Now, we subtract the discount amount from the initial price to find the final price. The final price is $900."

**Scene 4: Conclusion**

* Camera angle: Straight-on shot of the whiteboard or calculator
* Action: Display the final price ($900) on the whiteboard or calculator
* Voiceover: "Therefore, the final price of the product with a 10% discount is $900."

**Reasoning:**

1. The initial product price is $1000.
2. The discount percentage is 10% (or 0.10).
3. To find the discount amount, we multiply the initial price by the discount percentage: $1000 x 0.10 = $100.
4. To find the final price, we subtract the discount amount from the initial price: $1000 - $100 = $900.

**Final Answer:**

The final price of the product with a 10% discount is $900.
